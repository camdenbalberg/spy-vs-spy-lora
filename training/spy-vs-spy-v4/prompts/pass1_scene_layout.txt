WHAT IS A SCENE/SHOT:
A new scene/shot starts when the camera CUTS to a different view. This means any change in:
- Camera angle (even subtle shifts count)
- Framing (e.g., wide shot to medium shot, or full body to upper body)
- Location or setting
- Which characters are visible
- Zoom level (cutting from far to close or vice versa)

This animation uses rapid, frequent cuts — typically every 1-3 seconds. Even quick establishing shots or reaction shots that last only 1 second should be their own scene.

CRITICAL: Do NOT combine multiple shots into one row. If your action description contains words like "then", "and then", "before", "after", or semicolons joining separate actions, you are almost certainly combining multiple shots. Split them.
